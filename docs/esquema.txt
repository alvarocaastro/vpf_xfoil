3. METHODOLOGY

   3.1. General description of the procedure
        - Systematic approach in 6 stages
        - Automated Python pipeline
        - Reproducibility and traceability
        - Complete workflow

   3.2. Stage 1: Automatic aerofoil selection
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Candidates analysed (NACA 65-210, 65-410, 63-215, 0012)
          * Unified reference conditions
          * Combined scoring algorithm
        - Justification

   3.3. Stage 2: XFOIL aerodynamic simulations
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Flight conditions (Takeoff, Climb, Cruise, Descent)
          * Radial sections (root, mid-span, tip)
          * Characteristic Reynolds numbers
          * Ncrit parameter per condition
          * Angle-of-attack range
        - Justification
          * Reynolds variation along the blade span
          * Importance of multiple flight conditions

   3.4. Stage 3: Compressibility correction
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Prandtl-Glauert model
          * Target Mach numbers per condition
          * CL correction, conservative approximation for CD
        - Justification
          * Need to correct for compressibility effects
          * Model validity for subsonic flow

   3.5. Stage 4: Performance metrics calculation
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Maximum efficiency (CL/CD)max
          * Optimal angle of attack (second peak, α ≥ 3°)
          * Coefficients at optimal conditions
        - Justification of the second efficiency peak
          * Laminar separation bubble artefact
          * Relevant operating condition for turbomachinery

   3.6. Stage 5: Pitch kinematics and 3D fan analysis
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Cascade corrections (Weinig, Carter)
          * 3D rotational corrections (Snel)
          * Design twist and off-design span-wise trade-off
          * Stage loading: φ and ψ coefficients (Euler)
          * Velocity triangles: Va → φ → β_mech, Δβ per condition
        - Justification
          * The fan does not operate as an isolated aerofoil — cascade and rotation effects are decisive
          * Quantifies the fixed-pitch penalty at off-design conditions

   3.7. Stage 6: SFC impact analysis
        - Objective
        - Methodology
          * Comparison VPF (α_opt) vs fixed pitch (α_design) at same Mach/Re
          * Efficiency transfer model: ε, τ, k = BPR/(1+BPR)
          * Sensitivity sweep over τ
          * Baseline engine parameters (Trent/GE90 class)
        - Justification
          * Quantitative link between 2D/3D aerodynamic improvement and fuel consumption
          * Final TFG result: mean ΔSFC ≈ 2.5% within the literature range 2–5%
